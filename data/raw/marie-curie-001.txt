Marie Curie was born in Warsaw, Poland in 1867. She moved to Paris, France to study at the Sorbonne University. Together with her husband Pierre Curie, she discovered Polonium and Radium. Marie Curie won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911, making her the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences. She founded the Curie Institute in Paris and worked extensively on radioactivity research.
